## Full Transcript

**Jeremy Fleitz** [00:38](https://youtu.be/DFYtiTgKmPA?t=38)

Okay, welcome to wasmCloud Wednesday, the March 25 edition. This is going to be a very short one because a lot of us are at Amsterdam, EU KubeCon. I guess we'll start with that first — I know it's a little bit backwards from the agenda. A lot of people here on the call may have been at [Wasm I/O](https://wasmio.tech/) last week. We had an amazing wasmCloud event this past Monday — the videos for all the speakers will be posted shortly. So once those are available, we'll let everybody know.

Currently right now [EU KubeCon](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/kubecon-cloudnativecon-europe/) is happening, and the one overall thing to mention about that: **WebAssembly is definitely getting a lot more of the spotlight out there**. Naturally there are people that are just walking up to our Cosmonic booth and just asking questions, right out of the gate. The wasmCloud Project Pavilion booth was seeing a lot of people, new people as well — Eric on the call here was managing that.

The exciting thing for this week though is **wasmCloud v2 was finally released**. Actually it was **2.0.1** — we had to do an immediate release because we needed to do the updates of moving `wash` to the GitHub `wasmcloud/wasmcloud` repository and fix the builds to make sure everything was correct and properly tagged. So a lot of thanks to everybody that was involved with getting 2.0.1 out the door. The [documentation page for wasmCloud](https://wasmcloud.com/docs) has been updated so that the 2.0 documentation is the current, most up-to-date documentation to match the release.

So with that being said, this is going to be very short. Those are the announcements for today. Please check out v2 if you haven't yet, and we'll just go ahead and open up the floor to any questions or any new topics that people might want to talk about.

**Jeremy Fleitz** [03:04](https://youtu.be/DFYtiTgKmPA?t=184)

All right. Like I said, this is going to be a quick Wednesday. We will definitely have probably more to talk about next week after KubeCon's over and we get some sleep. I wish everybody a good, happy Wednesday and a good rest of the week.
