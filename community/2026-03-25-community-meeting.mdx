## Meeting Notes

### wasmCloud v2.0.1 Release

Jeremy Fleitz announced the **wasmCloud v2.0.1** release. The patch was cut quickly after v2.0 to move the `wash` CLI into the main [wasmCloud GitHub monorepo](https://github.com/wasmcloud/wasmcloud) and to fix some build and tagging issues that surfaced immediately after the v2.0 cut. Thanks to everyone who helped get the release out the door. The [wasmCloud documentation site](https://wasmcloud.com/docs/) has been updated to match — v2 docs are now the current, most up-to-date documentation.

### Wasmcon and KubeCon EU

Last Monday's **CNCF Wasmcon** in Amsterdam was a strong show. Videos from all of the speakers will be posted shortly; the team will share the link in the wasmCloud community channels once they're up. **KubeCon EU** is happening in Amsterdam this week (March 24-26), and **WebAssembly is visibly getting more attention** than in prior years — at the Cosmonic booth and at the CNCF wasmCloud Project Pavilion. People are walking up and asking informed questions about wasmCloud, not "what's Wasm?" — that shift, observed throughout the team this conference, is exactly what the v2 launch was timed for.

## WebAssembly News and Updates

This short call sits in the middle of a busy two-week run for the WebAssembly community: [Wasm I/O](https://wasmio.tech/) in Barcelona (March 19-20), [CNCF Wasmcon](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/wasmcon-europe/) in Amsterdam (March 23), and [KubeCon EU](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/kubecon-cloudnativecon-europe/) (March 24-26). The wasmCloud v2 launch was deliberately scheduled to coincide with this run so the broader [CNCF](https://www.cncf.io/projects/wasmcloud/) cloud-native community could see the new Kubernetes-native architecture in person. The [WebAssembly component model](https://component-model.bytecodealliance.org/) is meaningfully more present in cloud-native conversations than it was even six months ago.

## What is wasmCloud?

[wasmCloud](https://wasmcloud.com/) is a CNCF project for building and running [WebAssembly components](/docs/overview/workloads/components/) across cloud, edge, and Kubernetes. The v2 release referenced in this call is the major Kubernetes-native architecture that schedules wasmCloud hosts as standard pods, with workload deploys managed by the runtime operator, and inbound traffic reverse-proxied directly to host pods via the runtime gateway. Components are isolated through the [component model](/docs/overview/workloads/components/), distributed as OCI artifacts with built-in cosign / SIG store attestation, and observed via [OpenTelemetry](/docs/kubernetes-operator/operator-manual/overview/).
